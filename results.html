Consumption Risk Indicator

Ingredients Breakdown
High Fructose Corn-Syrup
Description: The primary sweetener in Coca-Cola in the U.S. is high-fructose corn syrup (HFCS), while in some other countries (e.g., Mexico), it is cane sugar.
Harmful Effects:
  • Obesity and Weight Gain
  • Diabetes
  • Tooth Decay
  • Metabolic Syndrome
Caramel Color (E150d)
Description: A coloring agent made by heating sugar, often with acids or alkalis, giving Coca-Cola its dark brown color.
Harmful Effects:
  • Carcinogen Risk
  • Allergy Risk
Phosphoric Acid
Description: An acid used to add a sharp, tangy flavor and act as a preservative.
Harmful Effects:
  • Tooth Erosion
  • Osteoporosis
Recommendations
  • Sodas are high in sugar and empty calories, which contribute to weight gain and increase the risk of obesity and type 2 diabetes. It's recommended to limit consumption to occasional treats rather than daily habits.
  • Drinking water regularly is essential. Soda doesn’t hydrate as well as water and can lead to dehydration, especially if consumed in large amounts.
  • The high sugar content and acidity in soda can cause tooth decay and enamel erosion. Brushing your teeth after drinking soda or using a straw can help minimize contact with your teeth.
Alternatives
  • Sparkling Water with Natural Flavors: Sparkling water provides the fizzy sensation of soda without the sugar and calories. You can add a slice of lemon, lime, or berries to infuse flavor naturally.
  • Coconut Water:Coconut water is naturally low in calories, contains essential electrolytes, and provides hydration.
  • Freshly Squeezed Lemonade: A homemade lemonade using minimal natural sweeteners like honey or agave can be refreshing and contains far less sugar than sodas.
  • Low-Sugar Electrolyte Drinks: Some low-sugar or sugar-free electrolyte drinks (like certain sports drinks) are great for hydration after workouts without the excessive sugars found in soda.
